### Discovery Questions (4 Minutes)

```
After demo, ask:

1. "Does this solve a problem you actually have?" (Yes/No)

2. "How much time would this save your team each week?" (Get specific number)

3. "What would your team DO with that time back?" (Find the real value)

4. "What would prevent you from using something like this?" (Surface objections early)

5. "If this worked exactly as I showed, would it be worth $999/month to you?" (Gauge budget)

6. "What would you need to see to make this a no-brainer?" (Feature gaps)
```

### Handling Objections

**Objection: "Our WMS should do this already"**

```
"You're right - ideally it would. But does it?

Most WMS systems are built by IT for IT. They're data dumps, not actionable intelligence.

This is built by a warehouse guy for warehouse guys. It speaks your language and shows you exactly what needs attention TODAY.

Want to try it for 30 days and see if it catches things your WMS isn't catching?"
```

---

**Objection: "Too expensive"**

```
"I get it. Let me break down the math:

If this saves your supervisor 15 hours per week, that's:
- 60 hours per month
- At $50/hour (loaded cost) = $3,000 per month saved

You're paying $999/month, saving $3,000/month.

That's a 3x ROI on labor savings alone - not counting the $5K-$50K pallet losses it prevents.

Does that math work for you?"
```

---

**Objection: "We need to think about it"**

```
"Totally fair. What specifically do you need to think about?

[Listen to real objection, then address it]

How about this: Let's schedule a follow-up for next week. Between now and then:
- I'll send you a detailed ROI breakdown
- You talk to your team
- You send me any questions

Sound good?"
```

---

**Objection: "We don't have budget right now"**

```
"When does your budget reset? (Get specific date)

And just to clarify: If you DID have budget today, would you buy this?"

[If yes]: "Okay, let's do a free pilot now, and we'll time the contract to start when your budget resets. That way you can show your CFO exactly how much you're saving when you request the budget."

[If no]: "Fair enough. What would need to change for this to be worth the investment?"
```
