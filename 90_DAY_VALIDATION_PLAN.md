## THE GOAL

**Primary Objective:** Get **3-5 warehouse managers** to say **"I will pay for this"** after seeing a demo.

**Success = Keep Going:**
- 3-5 qualified prospects commit to:
  - Paid pilot ($500-$999/month), OR
  - Free pilot with signed agreement to convert to paid within 30 days, OR
  - Clear "we'll buy this when X feature is added" (specific, not vague)

**Failure = Shut It Down:**
- Lots of "interesting but..." responses
- "We'll think about it" with no follow-up
- No one willing to commit even to a free pilot
- Less than 3 serious conversations about purchasing

#### Week 2: Prepare Your Pitch & Demo

**Day 8-10: Create Your 3-Minute Demo**

Your demo should follow this structure:

**1. The Hook (30 seconds)**
> "I'm going to show you something that turns 3 hours of daily inventory analysis into 30 seconds, and catches costly pallet losses before they become emergencies."

**2. The Problem (30 seconds)**
> "Right now, you or your team spend hours every day in Excel hunting for stagnant pallets, incomplete lots, overcapacity issues. And by the time you find them manually, they're already costing you money. Last week alone, how many hours did your team spend analyzing inventory reports?"

**3. The Demo (90 seconds)**
- Show: Upload a sample inventory CSV
- Show: System analyzes in 5 seconds
- Show: Dashboard with priority anomalies
- Show: Specific alerts (stagnant pallet in RECEIVING for 8 days, location overcapacity by 30%)
- Show: "This would have taken your supervisor 2-3 hours to find manually"

**4. The Value (30 seconds)**
> "This saves your team 15+ hours per week and catches problems like [specific example from demo] before they cost you $10K-$50K. We have warehouses using this daily who say it's caught issues they would have missed for weeks."

## THE MINDSET

### What to Expect

**Weeks 1-2:** Exciting (building, preparing, optimistic)
**Weeks 3-4:** Frustrating (low response rates, ghosting, rejections)
**Weeks 5-6:** Momentum building (demos happening, real conversations)
**Weeks 7-8:** Emotional rollercoaster (some yes, some no, uncertainty)
**Weeks 9-10:** Clarity emerging (patterns visible, sense of direction)
**Weeks 11-12:** Decision time (data tells you what to do)

### How to Stay Motivated

1. **Celebrate small wins:**
   - First response = win
   - First demo = win
   - First "this is interesting" = win
   - First objection (you're learning) = win

2. **Detach from outcome:**
   - You're testing a hypothesis, not proving you're smart
   - "No" is data, not personal rejection
   - Every conversation teaches you something

3. **Have an accountability buddy:**
   - Share your weekly metrics with someone
   - Schedule weekly check-ins
   - Someone who will call you out if you're slacking

4. **Remember the goal:**
   - You're not trying to build a $100M company in 90 days
   - You're testing: "Is there real demand?"
   - 3-5 commitments = proof of concept
   - That's ALL you need
